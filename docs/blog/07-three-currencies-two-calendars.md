# Software for a Life in Three Currencies and Two Calendars

- **Status:** outlined
- **Type:** human + technical
- **Serves:** personal + professional signal (distinctive)
- **Sources:** AutoSpendTracker (multi-currency), FabrikTakt (Jalali dates),
  your own path: Iran → Spain → Mexico → Germany
- **Length:** ~900 words
- **Why:** the most personal post; the immigrant-engineer throughline.

## Hook

I've lived on four countries' worth of paperwork. Somewhere along the way my
software started reflecting that: money in three currencies, dates in two
calendars, text in two directions. The edge cases in my life became the
edge cases in my code.

## Angle

A lot of "internationalization" is treated as a chore. When you actually live
across borders, it stops being a chore and becomes a design driver — and it
makes you a better engineer, because you stop assuming the defaults.

## Outline

- Multi-currency by necessity: MXN day-to-day, EUR at home, USD subscriptions
  — AutoSpendTracker existed because my money didn't live in one place.
- Two calendars: Jalali dates in FabrikTakt weren't a feature request, they
  were the reality of the users (and me).
- The broader lesson: every default (currency, date, locale, text direction,
  name format) is an assumption someone's life breaks.
- How living abroad rewires what "obvious" means in a data model.

## Takeaway

Your defaults are someone's edge case. If you've only built for one place,
one currency, one calendar — you've been coding with the training wheels on.
